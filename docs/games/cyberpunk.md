# Cyberpunk Red — How to Play

Night City, 2045. The Fourth Corporate War ended with a nuke in Arasaka Tower. The world is rebuilding — more chrome, more corps, less humanity. You're an edgerunner: Solo, Netrunner, Tech, Media, Fixer, Rockerboy, Cop, Medtech, Nomad, or Corporate. You take jobs. You survive. You try not to zero.

## The Character Sheet

Create through **⚙ Manage → Characters → + New Character**. Inkandbone rolls all nine stats (2d6+2), calculates Humanity from EMP, and gives you starting Eurodollars.

**The stats** are your raw capabilities: INT, REF, COOL, TECH, LK, ATT, MA, EMP, BODY. Each is rated 2-14. REF matters for combat. EMP determines your starting Humanity. BODY determines how much damage you can take.

**Humanity** = EMP × 10. Installing cyberware costs Humanity. Drop low enough and you develop cyberpsychosis. Your character changes.

**Eurodollars** is cash. You need it for gear, ammo, rent, and information.

**Role** determines your special ability: Solo has Combat Awareness, Netrunner jacks into the NET, Medtech has Pharma, etc.

## How the AI Runs Cyberpunk

The AI adjudicates Friday Night Firefight — the combat system. It's lethal. A single shot from a decent pistol does 3d6 damage. An unarmored person has around 30 HP. Do the math.

**Skill checks** are 1d10 + Skill Base + Stat Bonus vs a Difficulty Value. Simple is DV9, Everyday is DV13, Difficult is DV17, Very Difficult is DV21, Almost Impossible is DV29.

**Combat:** Initiative is REF + 1d10. On your turn, you get Move + Action. Ranged attacks are your skill + REF vs DV15. Damage is weapon damage minus armor SP (Stopping Power). Damage that gets through is dealt to HP.

**Armor** degrades when it stops damage. Each hit reduces SP by 1. Repair it between jobs.

**Humanity Loss** from cyberware installation is immediate and permanent (unless you get therapy). Track it — low EMP changes your personality. The AI narrates this.

## Playing

Open the browser. Get your character and session active. The fixer has a job for you:

> *I push through the crowd at the Afterlife, scanning for the fixer everyone calls Twitchy. A man in a battered leather jacket meets my eye from a corner booth. That's him.*

The AI runs the meet — negotiation, details, complications. Then legwork — gather information, case the location, buy equipment. Then the job — execute, extract, get paid.

## What a Run Looks Like

**The Meet:** Your fixer (or a Mr. Johnson) gives you the job. Learn the target, the constraints, the pay. Negotiate. The AI sets the stakes.

**Legwork:** Gather information. Streetwise to find contacts, Perception to case the location, Stealth if you're scouting. The more you learn, the less the job surprises you.

**The Job:** Execute. Retrieve something, eliminate someone, protect a target, sabotage a location. One firefight is enough for a first session.

**The Payday:** Payment, complications, reputation changes. The next job hooks from this one's fallout.

## Rules to Remember

- **d10 explodes:** When you roll a 10, roll again and add. This keeps even low-skilled characters dangerous.
- **Luck points:** Spend 1 LUCK to add 1 to any roll after you see the result. They refresh each session.
- **Armor SP degrades:** Every hit your armor stops reduces its SP by 1. Replace or repair it.
- **Critical Injuries:** A weapon's maximum damage roll (or damage that exceeds your HP in one hit) causes a critical injury. The AI rolls on the crit table.
- **Role Abilities:** Each role has a unique ability. Tell the AI yours early so it can offer you opportunities to use it.

# Dune: Adventures in the Imperium — How to Play

You serve a Great House. Your lord has sent you to Arrakis — or to the Palace, or to a Richese factory, or to a Spacing Guild Heighliner — to advance the House's position. Every decision affects your House's standing. Every failure is a blow to your family's power. Every success is measured in spice.

Dune uses the 2d20 system. You roll d20s against your skill rating. Your **Drives** (personal motivations) generate bonus dice. The AI tracks **Momentum** (a shared player resource) and **Threat** (the GM's counterpart), and they flow back and forth like the political struggle the game is named for.

## The Character Sheet

Create through **⚙ Manage → Characters → + New Character**. Inkandbone picks an Archetype (20 options), assigns Drives (one 8, one 4, rest 6), and sets skills based on your Archetype's primary/secondary.

**Drives** are pip dots (max 10): Duty, Faith, Justice, Power, Truth. These are your character's core motivations. When an action aligns with a Drive, add the Drive's rating in bonus d20s to your pool. Acting against a Drive costs you.

**Skills** are number inputs: Battle, Communicate, Discipline, Move, Understand. Each rated 4-7. Your Archetype determines which is primary and secondary.

**Determination** is your heroic resource (starts at 2). Spend 1 to guarantee success on a roll critical to your Drive or Ambition, or to survive something lethal. These are rare.

**Focuses** are areas of expertise — "Swordplay", "Spice Negotiation", "Bene Gesserit Truthsense". They reduce difficulty on related rolls.

**Wounds** is your health. At 0, you're incapacitated. Healing requires medical attention.

**Advancement** is XP. Spend it to raise Drives and skills.

## The 2d20 System

Every risky action works the same way:

1. The AI sets a **difficulty** (1-5 successes needed)
2. Your **dice pool** is 2 base d20s + bonus dice from relevant Drives, Momentum spends, assets, or Determination
3. Roll each die. Each equal to or below your relevant **skill rating** is a success
4. Successes that exceed the difficulty become **Momentum**

**Momentum** is a shared pool (max 6). Anyone can spend 1 Momentum to:
- Add a bonus d20 to any roll
- Reduce difficulty by 1
- Create a narrative detail ("I planted a listening device earlier")
- Introduce a fact that benefits the party

Momentum resets between scenes. Use it or lose it.

**Threat** is the AI's pool. When you fail a roll, the AI gains Threat equal to the shortfall. The AI spends Threat to:
- Add complications to scenes
- Boost NPC stats
- Introduce environmental hazards
- Trigger consequences from previous failures

## Playing

Open the browser. Get your session active. Your House has sent you on a mission:

> *I approach the Baron's audience chamber with measured steps, my House signet visible on my gloved hand. I bow precisely three degrees — not too deep, not too shallow. "My Lord Baron, I bring word from my House regarding the spice quota discrepancy on Arrakis."*

The AI describes the chamber, the Baron's reaction, the aides in the room, the undercurrents of the conversation. When you make your case, the AI sets a difficulty and you roll.

If you succeed, you gain Momentum — use it to extract more information, secure a better deal, or set up your next move. If you fail, the AI gains Threat — expect complications later.

## The Mission

**The Briefing:** Your House learns of an opportunity or a threat. A Mentat analyzes the situation. A Bashar recommends a course of action. Your lord gives you your mission.

**The Approach:** Travel to the location of your mission. Use Communicate for negotiation, Move for physical travel, Discipline for endurance. The AI presents obstacles that test your Drives.

**The Confrontation:** The key scene — a negotiation, a duel, an infiltration, a discovery. Your primary Drive is tested. The outcome shapes your House's position.

**The Aftermath:** Report back. Your House's resources change. Your personal Drive may be fulfilled or challenged. The next mission emerges.

## Key Rules

- **2d20 core:** Roll d20s. Each at or under your skill = 1 success. Meet the difficulty to succeed.
- **Drives add dice:** When an action serves a Drive, add that Drive in bonus d20s.
- **Momentum:** Excess successes become Momentum (max 6). Spend it to help future rolls or create advantages.
- **Threat:** The AI gets Threat when you fail. Threat escalates situations.
- **Determination:** 2 per character. Spend to guarantee a critical success or survive. They don't come back often.
- **Wounds:** At 0, you're down. Healing takes time and medical skill.
- **The Spice Must Flow:** Water is life. Trust is earned. Every conversation has layers. Roleplay accordingly.

# Dune: Adventures in the Imperium — First Adventure Walkthrough

## System Overview

Dune: Adventures in the Imperium is a tabletop RPG set in Frank Herbert's Dune universe — a feudal interstellar empire where noble Houses maneuver for power on Arrakis, the only source of the spice melange. You play agents of a Great House or a faction of the Imperium — assassins, mentats, Bene Gesserit, smugglers, or Fremen — navigating politics, desert survival, and the complex 2d20 resolution system. The AI acts as your Game Master, narrating the halls of power and the deep desert, voicing noble rivals and Sardaukar commanders, and adjudicating the intricate social and tactical mechanics of the 2d20 system.

## Character Creation

Characters for Dune are built from the schema-driven character sheet. Key fields include Archetype (your role in the Imperium), Drive (personal motivation), the six 2d20 attributes (Awareness, Befitting, Cunning, Resolve, Strength, and Wits), skills (20 total covering combat, social, and technical domains), and resource tracks for the three critical meters — Lifeblood (health), Momentum, and Threat. Your character's House affiliation and faction determine starting assets and narrative hooks.

## Key Mechanic

The 2d20 system uses two d20s rolled against a target number derived from attribute + skill. Rolls determine number of successes: each die at or below the target is a success. Bonus dice from advantages and Momentum can be banked for future rolls. The AI tracks all pools and modifiers automatically.

# Ironsworn — How to Play

You are a vow-bound hero in the Ironlands — a harsh frontier of misty valleys, ancient ruins, and untold dangers. You make promises (Iron Vows) and pursue them across a dangerous world. The AI is your Oracle and Guide. It interprets your moves, rolls the challenge dice, and narrates what happens.

Ironsworn is designed for solo play. You drive the story. The AI reacts, surprises you, and throws obstacles in your path. There is no planned plot — it emerges from your vows and how you pursue them.

## The Character Sheet

Create through **⚙ Manage → Characters → + New Character**. Inkandbone distributes 7 points across five attributes (each 1-3) and sets Health, Spirit, Supply to 5, Momentum to 2.

**Edge, Heart, Iron, Shadow, Wits** — the five attributes. Each is rated 1-3. You add your relevant stat to your action die (d6) when making a move.

**Health** is your physical condition. Hit 0 and you're in trouble. **Spirit** is your morale. Low Spirit makes everything harder. **Supply** is your equipment and provisions. Run out and you're in the wild with nothing.

**Momentum** is your narrative advantage. Build it high (up to 10) before attempting risky moves. When your action die comes up low, burn Momentum to replace it. A miss resets Momentum to 2.

**Vows** are your sworn promises — the things that drive your story. Each vow has a progress track.

## How Play Works

You describe what your character does. The AI identifies the **move** you're making, asks for the relevant stat, and interprets the dice.

> *I stand in the rain-soaked ruins of my village. A figure moves in the treeline. I grip my axe. "Show yourself!"*

The AI responds: this sounds like **Face Danger** using your Heart or Iron. Roll your action die (d6) + the stat. The AI rolls two challenge dice (d10s) alongside.

Your action die + stat = your result. Compare it to both challenge dice:
- **Strong hit:** Beat both. You succeed completely. Gain +1 Momentum.
- **Weak hit:** Beat one. You succeed at a cost. The AI tells you the price.
- **Miss:** Beat neither. Things get worse. The AI introduces a complication. Momentum resets to 2.

## The Vow Progression

Everything in Ironsworn revolves around vows:
1. **Swear an Iron Vow** — make the vow roll. The result determines how hard it will be to fulfill.
2. **Pursue the vow** — each session, make progress toward your goal. Every strong or weak hit in pursuit ticks your progress track.
3. **Fulfill Your Vow** — when the progress track is full, make the fulfillment roll. Strong hit: vow fulfilled. Weak hit: fulfilled but at a cost. Miss: you failed. The vow remains unfulfilled.

## Key Moves

- **Face Danger** — overcome an obstacle with the relevant stat
- **Secure an Advantage** — set up a future action by being clever
- **Gather Information** — learn something about the world
- **Heal** — recover Health using your Wits
- **Resupply** — recover Supply using your Wits
- **Sojourn** — rest in a safe settlement to recover
- **Swear an Iron Vow** — commit to a quest
- **Reach Your Destination** — complete a journey
- **Compel** — persuade or intimidate someone
- **Clash** — fight back in combat
- **Strike** — attack in combat

Ask the AI to name the move as you play. It helps you learn the system.

## Starting

A typical start: your character returns to their village and finds it destroyed. Survivors are scattered. The attackers left a trail. You swear an Iron Vow to find who did this and make them pay.

From there: follow the trail (Gather Information, Face Danger), reach the raiders' camp (Undertake a Journey), confront the leader (Clash, Strike, or Compel). Each move makes the story.

## Tips

- Your Momentum is your most powerful tool. Build it before risky moves, burn it when you need to.
- A miss is not failure — it's a complication that makes the story more interesting.
- Tell the AI your stat values so it can structure its responses around your strengths and weaknesses.
- Ask the AI to consult the Oracle when you're uncertain about the world. It rolls narrative tables for inspiration.
- Describe how your character feels about each setback. Ironsworn rewards emotional depth.

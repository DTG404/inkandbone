# Shadowrun (6th Edition) — How to Play

2080. Megacorporations rule the world. Magic has returned — elves, orks, dwarves, and trolls walk alongside humans. The Matrix is a digital universe layered over reality. You are a shadowrunner — a deniable asset hired to do things the corps can't officially sanction.

## Your Character Sheet

Create through **⚙ Manage → Characters → + New Character**. Inkandbone rolls your attributes at 1d6 each, sets Essence to 6, rolls Edge at 1d4, and calculates your three limits.

**Attributes** are the core: Body, Agility, Reaction, Strength, Willpower, Logic, Intuition, Charisma. Each is rated 1-6 (or higher for metahumans). These are what you add to skills to build dice pools.

**Edge** is your metacurrency. Spend 1 to add 2 dice, reroll failures, or go first in initiative. Gain Edge by playing to your strengths or exploiting enemy weaknesses. It refreshes each scene.

**Essence** reflects your humanity. Start at 6. Cyberware and bioware reduce it. Low Essence makes magic harder and changes how the world sees you.

**Limits** (Physical, Mental, Social) cap how many hits you can keep from a dice pool. They're derived from your attributes.

**Karma** is advancement. Spend it between runs to raise attributes and skills.

## The Three Worlds

Shadowrun operates on three layers. The AI handles all of them.

**Physical** — the real world. Streets, buildings, guns, people. This is where the samurai and the infiltrator operate.

**Matrix** — the digital world. Deckers and Technomancers jack in and navigate hosts, IC, and data. The AI runs Matrix combat as a parallel scene — the decker fights in cyberspace while the team fights physically.

**Astral** — the spirit world. Mages and adepts perceive and project onto the astral plane. Spirits, mana barriers, and astral signatures are visible here. The AI narrates astral encounters for awakened characters.

## Playing

Open the browser. Get your team loaded. A fixer has set up a meeting:

> *I'm jacked into the host through my datajack. The host's iconography is a sterile corporate lobby. I start a Matrix Search using my Computer 6 — looking for the file.*

The AI resolves the action and describes the result. For Matrix action, it runs the host architecture floor-by-floor. For physical action, it describes the environment and NPCs. The AI tracks both layers simultaneously.

## The Run Structure

**The Meet:** Mr. Johnson offers the job at a bar, a noodle shop, or a park at midnight. Negotiate pay using Con + Charisma. Ask about complications — the AI hints at what's not being said.

**Legwork:** Research. Matrix searches (Computer + Logic). Street contacts (Con + Charisma). Astral recon (Assensing + Intuition). Each piece of information reduces risk in the execution phase.

**The Run:** Execute the plan. One major obstacle per scene — physical security, IC in the Matrix, a spirit guardian. Extract with the objective.

**The Payoff:** Mr. Johnson pays (probably). Karma is awarded. Reputation changes. The next run hooks from this one's loose ends.

## Key Rules

- **Dice pools:** Build Attribute + Skill in d6s. Each 5-6 is a hit. Compare hits to a threshold.
- **Glitches:** If half or more of your dice show 1, it's a glitch — complication even if you succeed. Zero hits + glitch = critical glitch (disaster).
- **Edge economy:** Spend Edge for +2 dice, reroll failures, or interrupt initiative. Earn it back by playing to your archetype.
- **Noise:** Matrix dice pools are reduced by noise (distance, jamming, spam zones). Ask the AI for the noise rating before jacking in.
- **Drain:** Casting spells causes drain — physical or mental damage based on the spell's power. The AI tracks it.
- **Limits:** You can only keep a number of hits equal to your relevant limit. Edge spending can temporarily ignore this.
